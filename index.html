Paraná, Brazil

Paraná is a state located in the southern region of Brazil. It borders São Paulo to the north, Santa Catarina to the south, and Mato Grosso do Sul to the west. It also touches the Atlantic Ocean to the east. The state is known for its geographical diversity, including mountains, plains, and vast forests, and is an important agricultural and industrial hub in the country. Curitiba, the capital and largest city, is renowned for its innovative urban planning and serves as a cultural, educational, and economic center. Paraná is also famous for its natural beauty, including the Iguaçu Falls, one of Brazil's top tourist attractions.
